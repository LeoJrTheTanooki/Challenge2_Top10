Top 10 Underrated Indie Games

#10: Harvest Hero Origins


Starting off with something simple, Harvest Hero Origins throws the player back to the third generation of consoles with its 8-bit graphics and simple-yet-addicting gameplay. In this game you play as one of five heroes to defend the village of Fronvale from an army of Grooble, with each hero having a different playstyle, and different ways of approaching oncoming enemies. You can also destroy oncoming boulders that contain power-ups such as a fire ring to destroy anything near you, and as the game goes on you'll be blessed with new moves to make defending the village much easier. Of course as the game implies, you can also harvest any vegetables growing on the battlefield… they don't do much, but you can harvest them for additional points if you wish to upload your score to the leaderboard. As for content, the game offers a survival mode for the player to either go solo, or to have a friend join in to help clear out hordes of enemies charging towards the village, and a story mode, telling the tale of the titular Harvest Hero. If you're into something like The Legend of Zelda on NES with its combat, and/or you just so happen to have a friend nearby, you'll be sure to have a good time with this game. Of course however, the game does have Origins in it's title, and it's not just there for show either, as this is just the beginning for what Gemdrop Games has to offer, though this game will be what they have to show for the time being, as they are actively developing their next title.

The game is available on Steam and itch.io.

#9: Carrie's Order Up!


Imagine Pac-Mania, but your goal is to serve the ghosts food without bumping into them, that's Carrie's Order Up in a nutshell. You play as a crab waiter named Carrie devoted to serving customers their meals, so much so that she won't stop for anything… not even for other customers. That however isn't a huge issue for her, as she's able to spin her way around the customers should she be on a collision course towards them, all while keeping any food OR plates stable, and there's an extra emphasis on that OR for a reason, because Carrie can only carry either food to serve to customers OR plates left behind to wash in the sink for extra cash. The big thing here is to avoid falling to the ground from bumping into customers, spinning over puddles, or slipping on banana peels, but it's not the end of the world if you fall for any reason as you can get rid of a miss by collecting enough coins that the customers drop… oh yeah I should also mention that the game uses a miss system rather than a lives system. The game is also generous enough to lend you power-ups to get through your job, like a mirror to phase through customers, and roller skates to increase your speed temporarily. This game also has a story mode where you play through a set assortment of levels that ramp up in difficulty, while showing the lives of Carrie the Crab Waiter and Calcia the Lobster Cook in-between checkpoints. The developer, inkDragonWorks, is also actively developing another game called Poppin & Jupa: Pocket Adventure, so definitely check him out when you get a chance.

The game is available on Steam and itch.io.

#8: The Unlikely Legend of Rusty Pup


All our entries have been quite bright in color and tone so far, haven't they? Get ready for some whiplash (kinda). The Unlikely Legend of Rusty Pup takes you to a Gothic and grungy steampunk world made of scrap metal with the sole task of making sure that a lone robot dog makes it through this unforgiving environment. However something important to note is that the game doesn't let you control the dog itself, instead you play as some sort of magic hand that has the ability to push-and-pull all sorts of contraptions to create paths for this robo-pup to safely traverse through, which includes making sure the little one doesn't fall from too great of a height and ensuring that it has enough fuel to last for the level. A major positive in this game is that you have to pet the dog in order to wake it up for the level to start, and if you disagree with that, you're just statistically and scientifically wrong. It's is a very welcome addition to the indie space even if often overlooked. If you've enjoyed games like Lemmings, Ghost Trick, or even the Mario vs. Donkey Kong games post-GBA, I'd say give this game a try if you haven't. If any of that hasn't convinced you… well… you know Rareware, right? And you know Conker's Bad Fur Day, right? The man behind that game, Chris Seavor, is the man behind this game as well.

The game is available on Steam, though it's worth noting that it was planned for the Nintendo 3DS and Wii U only for those to be canned, and the Switch version is vaporware at this point.
