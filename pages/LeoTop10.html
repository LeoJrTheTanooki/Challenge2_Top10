Top 10 Underrated Indie Games

#10: Harvest Hero Origins


Starting off with something simple, Harvest Hero Origins throws the player back to the third generation of consoles with its 8-bit graphics and simple-yet-addicting gameplay. In this game you play as one of five heroes to defend the village of Fronvale from an army of Grooble, with each hero having a different playstyle, and different ways of approaching oncoming enemies. You can also destroy oncoming boulders that contain power-ups such as a fire ring to destroy anything near you, and as the game goes on you'll be blessed with new moves to make defending the village much easier. Of course as the game implies, you can also harvest any vegetables growing on the battlefieldâ€¦ they don't do much, but you can harvest them for additional points if you wish to upload your score to the leaderboard. As for content, the game offers a survival mode for the player to either go solo, or to have a friend join in to help clear out hordes of enemies charging towards the village, and a story mode, telling the tale of the titular Harvest Hero. Of course however, the game does have Origins in it's title, and it's not just there for show either, as this is just the beginning for what Gemdrop Games has to offer, though this game will be what they have to show for the time being.
