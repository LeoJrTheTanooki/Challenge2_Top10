Top 10 Underrated Indie Games

#10: Harvest Hero Origins


Starting off with something simple, Harvest Hero Origins throws the player back to the third generation of consoles with its 8-bit graphics and simple-yet-addicting gameplay. In this game you play as one of five heroes to defend the village of Fronvale from an army of Grooble, with each hero having a different playstyle, and different ways of approaching oncoming enemies. You can also destroy oncoming boulders that contain power-ups such as a fire ring to destroy anything near you, and as the game goes on you'll be blessed with new moves to make defending the village much easier. Of course as the game implies, you can also harvest any vegetables growing on the battlefield… they don't do much, but you can harvest them for additional points if you wish to upload your score to the leaderboard. As for content, the game offers a survival mode for the player to either go solo, or to have a friend join in to help clear out hordes of enemies charging towards the village, and a story mode, telling the tale of the titular Harvest Hero. If you're into something like The Legend of Zelda on NES with its combat, and/or you just so happen to have a friend nearby, you'll be sure to have a good time with this game. Of course however, the game does have Origins in it's title, and it's not just there for show either, as this is just the beginning for what Gemdrop Games has to offer, though this game will be what they have to show for the time being, as they are actively developing their next title.

The game is available on Steam and itch.io.

#9: Carrie's Order Up!


Imagine Pac-Mania, but your goal is to serve the ghosts food without bumping into them, that's Carrie's Order Up in a nutshell. You play as a crab waiter named Carrie devoted to serving customers their meals, so much so that she won't stop for anything… not even for other customers. That however isn't a huge issue for her, as she's able to spin her way around the customers should she be on a collision course towards them, all while keeping any food OR plates stable, and there's an extra emphasis on that OR for a reason, because Carrie can only carry either food to serve to customers OR plates left behind to wash in the sink for extra cash. The big thing here is to avoid falling to the ground from bumping into customers, spinning over puddles, or slipping on banana peels, but it's not the end of the world if you fall for any reason as you can get rid of a miss by collecting enough coins that the customers drop… oh yeah I should also mention that the game uses a miss system rather than a lives system. The game is also generous enough to lend you power-ups to get through your job, like a mirror to phase through customers, and roller skates to increase your speed temporarily. This game also has a story mode where you play through a set assortment of levels that ramp up in difficulty, while showing the lives of Carrie the Crab Waiter and Calcia the Lobster Cook in-between checkpoints. The developer, inkDragonWorks, is also actively developing another game called Poppin & Jupa: Pocket Adventure, so definitely check him out when you get a chance.

The game is available on Steam and itch.io.

#8: The Unlikely Legend of Rusty Pup


All our entries have been quite bright in color and tone so far, haven't they? Get ready for some whiplash (kinda). The Unlikely Legend of Rusty Pup takes you to a Gothic and grungy steampunk world made of scrap metal with the sole task of making sure that a lone robot dog makes it through this unforgiving environment. However something important to note is that the game doesn't let you control the dog itself, instead you play as some sort of magic hand that has the ability to push-and-pull all sorts of contraptions to create paths for this robo-pup to safely traverse through, which includes making sure the little one doesn't fall from too great of a height and ensuring that it has enough fuel to last for the level. A major positive in this game is that you have to pet the dog in order to wake it up for the level to start, and if you disagree with that, you're just statistically and scientifically wrong. It's is a very welcome addition to the indie space even if often overlooked. If you've enjoyed games like Lemmings, Ghost Trick, or even the Mario vs. Donkey Kong games post-GBA, I'd say give this game a try if you haven't. If any of that hasn't convinced you… well… you know Rareware, right? And you know Conker's Bad Fur Day, right? The man behind that game, Chris Seavor, is the man behind this game as well.

The game is available on Steam, though it's worth noting that it was planned for the Nintendo 3DS and Wii U only for those to be canned, and the Switch version is vaporware at this point.

#7: Cool Kid Cody

Don't let the game's engine fool you, this is more than just some visual novel, it's cooler because now it's a point-and-click adventure. Cool Kid Cody is a game where you play as the local cool kid, Cody, in a world where cool people and losers are considered separate, gotta love how the game pretty much introduces this world's version of classicism in its opening. Joking aside, the game has a lot of charm to it, with its neon palette and funny dialogue when interacting with various objects in the world. The general gist of the game is that in your cool school, you're assigned a loser named PB, that pizza boy you see in the art, for the Chill Assignment Test and that's really about as far as I know about the plot and therefore a good ending point on talking about the plot. Admittedly I haven't gotten far into the game, but I'm already somewhat hooked on it, certainly helps that I played another game made by the same team, Purrfect Apawcalypse, a game where you try to make friends while trying not to die, but we're not talking about that game right now. Speaking of the same team however, the game's art style was even done by one of Jaiden Animations' team members, 90% Denny, so if you're into Jaiden, you may want to try something one of her animators made. Overall, the dialogue is good and so is the art style, and though I haven't gotten to really know the characters, I feel like I'll really start to get attached to some of them. The game is separated into episodes and seasons somewhat like the Telltale Games before they went under, although the first episode of every season is free to play, so if you just want to try the game out you're free to do so. If you like the odd dialogue of the EarthBound games and other Motherlikes, and/or you enjoy point-and-click adventures, you should at least play the first episode of season 1, since it's already free to play. Also consider checking out more of what 90% Studios does with art being done by Denny and programming being done by Andy.

The game is available on Steam and itch.io.

#6: Dragon Drop

To be specific, the one made by Cloudy Games LLC, there are two different games named Dragon Drop. With that out of the way, Dragon Drop is a game where you play as what looks like a dragon you'd see out of a Kirby game, called a pico dragon. When starting a game you can choose your pico dragon and what they wear, and you have the ability to either breathe fire and send blocks beneath falling, or taunt for the heck of it, and something happens when you press taunt next to another pico dragon. You also have a variety of stages to choose from, consisting of various shapes to avoid falling from. The general goal of the game is dependent on the game mode, with the main and first one being Classic Stock, so we'll go over that real quick. In Classic Stock, everyone is given five lives, and you all compete to be the last one standing as you send blocks falling with your fire breath, the more players that strike out, the smaller the battlefield gets, so you have to be careful constantly. And like I said there are other game modes to try out like Earthquake, Quickdraw, Rapture, and Spleef, so if you get tired of Classic, there's other modes to try out. If you like Bomberman, Mario Party, Gang Beasts, really any free-for-all game you would play with friends, have a get together and try this game out. The developers are hard at work at this game, and if you've ever gotten a charity itch.io bundle from itch themselves, chances are this game is one of the many games included in said bundle, though I'd say donate to the game itself to support it if you really do like it, it helps the developer to finish it.

The game is Early Access on Steam and itch.io.

#5: Frogun

Looks like the evolution of pixel art indies has finally arrived, with Frogun being one of a bunch of games with a low-poly art-style. In this game you play as a girl who sets out to save her parents using her primary tool and friend, the Frogun. You'll go through all sorts of levels, solving puzzles and collecting gems. Something worth noting is that you are rewarded for going through each level within a certain condition, such as getting to the goal before X amount of time, with all the gems collected, without getting hit, and whatnot, but don't worry about doing all that in one go, once you meet a threshold, that reward will be saved and added onto additional revisits of the levels. The game also allows you to change filters to better suit the game, so whether you want to play in crisp HD to see every detail or through some fuzzy scanlines to take you back is up to you. If you like games like Ape Escape, you should give Frogun a try. The developer, Molegato is even making a sequel, Frogun Encore, so you should check that out as well when it releases.

The game is available on Steam, Epic Games, Nintendo Switch, PS4, and Xbox One.

#4: Goodboy Galaxy

Do you want to go to space? Do you want to explore all sorts of planets? Do you want to make all sorts of friends along the way? And do you want to do all of that as a dog? Then you should know more about this game because it has all that and more. Goodboy Galaxy is a game where you're the last hope your planet has, as you seek out to the stars to find some help for your home. “How do you do that?” you may ask, well by gathering connections of course, and weapons as well because though you get to explore planets and meet new friends, there are plenty of creatures out there that are hostile and wouldn't exactly be considered friends. That's really all that I know about this game, considering the fact that it hasn't exactly released yet, however there is a demo to try out and to get a feel of the game. The neat thing about the demo, and the game in general however, is that it is all playable on the Game Boy Advance on actual hardware, with physical cartridges being available to preorder, or just the ROM to play through a flash cartridge or emulator. What the demo shows is an incredibly solid game where you traverse a cave with certain gear of yours being taken away temporarily, like exploring without your blaster, rendering you unable to shoot, or exploring without a jet-pack, making it very difficult to jump. The demo is simple and short, but does show what the game is about, and there's even a guest appearance from another game being made on the NES called Orange Island. If you're into games like Metroid or Cave Story, I'm sure you'll have a good time with this game. So why put this game on this list if it hasn't released? Well it's set to release winter of this year (2023). It's already October as of writing this, so the release day is swiftly approaching. I simply wanted to bring more attention to this game.

The demo is out on itch.io and the game is set to release on there alongside Steam, Nintendo Switch, the Evercade and the Game Boy Advance.

#3: Guacamelee!

Ever wanted to play as a super-powered luchador fighting poncho-wearing skeletons alongside a guardian named after some Mexican food while hearing all sorts of trumpets along the way? That sounds stereotypical, but you'd be wrong, that's just another Tuesday for Mexico, just ask any Mexican, mi tio would never lie about stuff like that. Guacamelee! is a game where you do all of what I mentioned earlier with the sole goal of saving El Presidente's daughter, a childhood friend of yours, from the clutches of Carlos Calaca, who plans on merging the land of the living and land of the dead to rule over both realms.. No one is exactly happy with this plan, not even El Diablo de la Biblia, so it's your job to stop the merge from happening. You do this by gathering new moves to get past obstacles blocking your progress and by combo-ing the hell out of anything that stands in your way with souped up luchador moves. The game can also be played with a second player, and with an updated re-release called the “Super Turbo Chanpionship Edition”, you can even play with a maximum of four players to send everything flying with your skills. If you liked Dust: An Elysian Tail, Guacamelee! is sure to satisfy your desire to combo the living hell out of everything. There's also a sequel simply titled Guacamelee! 2, which despite improving upon the first game, is still a direct sequel to it, and I'd recommend finishing that game first to really enjoy what the sequel throws at you.

The game is available on Steam, Epic Games, Nintendo Switch, PS4, and Xbox One. It even got a Vita release if you're curious.

#2: Rivals Of Aether

Look, I may be friends with furries, but there's more reason as to why I put this game here. Rivals of Aether is a platform fighter where you have 18 characters to choose from, two of which are Ori and Shovel Knight. I can't say whether it plays like Melee, but it definitely pulls techniques from competitive Melee and makes them much more fleshed out, and they're not only fleshed out either, the game also trains the player on how to use said techniques. The best part being that the techniques are easier to pull off, let me tell you it's fun to just wavedash with no real purpose other than the fact that I can. It has a story mode, but you can tell it was made during the very early days of the game, with new lore being spread among promo material and four comics. With all that being said, I would recommend it for being fresh alone, however the game has a very active Steam Workshop, allowing for entirely custom characters made (mostly) from the ground up, new stages to play, and buddies that can either watch you battle or change the game up in a way. If you like Super Smash Bros, Melee in particular, this games does try to hit close to home with that, with different mechanics to make it more of its own thing and for competitiveness's sake. There's, once again, a sequel in the works set to release soon as well from Aether studios, making the game 3D and hopefully fleshing out the characters more.

The game is available on Steam, Nintendo Switch, and Xbox One. Worth noting that the PC version is the most supported version and one again has Steam Workshop support, while the Xbox version has not gotten an update in a long time (funny given Dan Fornace, the creator, used to work for them)

#1: Grapple Dog

This game is not my personal favorite game of all time, but absolutely one that deserves more attention. In this game you play as a dog named Pablo, who traverses through levels using a grappling hook and a few moves of your own while fighting robots terrorizing various islands. Why are the robots terrorizing everyone, well… It's kinda your fault, but not really at the same time. After falling from a bridge you find a robot head who seems harmless enough and even gives you the grappling hook you use throughout the game, however after you help him out, he reveals his true intentions on destroying everything and even tries to kill you by throwing you into the ocean. You of course survive, but now it's up to you, your archaeological mentor, and your mechanical friend to stop the robot menace from destroying everything. The gameplay is especially solid, but the music really ramps things up, taking heavy inspiration from Jet Set Radio. The soundtrack really sets the tone of the game, as you travel through various levels and solve puzzles to collect gems in order to fight the bosses of each world. If you like Bionic Commando and/or Hideki Naganuma's music, this game has the grappling hooks and funky beats to keep you hooked. Another sequel soon too. By Medallion Games. Space this time. You'll most likely enjoy it if you like the first one.

The game is available on Steam, Nintendo Switch, and Xbox Series (Odd that there's no One version...).
